Samantha Dana

Professional History

I discovered a love of data through an internship and part-time gig at the National Priorities Project, a non-profit dedicated to making the federal budget accessible to every day citizens. I've continued my data work as a Grants Researcher at Springfield Technical Community College, where I find, analyze, and prepare federal, state, and local demographic and labor market data for inclusion in grant proposals, among other duties.


Education

I began my academic career as a German major at the former Las Vegas Academy of International Studies, Performing, and Visual Arts, a magnet high school in my hometown of Las Vegas. I continued on to the University of Nevada - Las Vegas, majoring in anthropology, and finished with a bachelor's degree in Political Science from the University of Massachusetts - Amherst. I am currently a student in the Master of Science in Government Analytics degree program at The Johns Hopkins University, concentrating in Statistical Analysis.


Extracurriculars

When I'm not working at STCC or on grad school work, I like to sing (either karaoke or with the Rock Voices choir), read (find me on Goodreads), travel, and hang out with my husband and two cats.
